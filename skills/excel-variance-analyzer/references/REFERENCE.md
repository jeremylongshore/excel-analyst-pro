# Variance Analysis Best Practices

## Overview

Variance analysis compares actual financial results against budgets or forecasts to identify discrepancies, understand their causes, and drive corrective action. This guide covers sign conventions, materiality thresholds, commentary frameworks, and executive reporting standards.

## Sign Conventions

### Revenue & Profit Line Items
- **Favorable**: Actual > Budget (positive variance)
- **Unfavorable**: Actual < Budget (negative variance)

### Expense Line Items
- **Favorable**: Actual < Budget (negative variance = under budget)
- **Unfavorable**: Actual > Budget (positive variance = over budget)

### Display Convention
- Favorable: Show as positive number (green)
- Unfavorable: Show in parentheses or with negative sign (red)
- Example: Revenue +$50K (favorable), OpEx ($30K) (unfavorable)

## Materiality Thresholds

### Standard Thresholds
| Level | Percentage | Absolute Amount | Action |
|-------|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Critical | >10% | >$100K | Immediate investigation, executive attention |
| Warning | 5-10% | $50K-$100K | Department head review, root cause analysis |
| Monitor | 2-5% | $25K-$50K | Note in report, track trend |
| On Track | <2% | <$25K | No action required |

### Threshold Customization
- **Startups**: Higher % thresholds (15-20%) due to forecast uncertainty
- **Mature businesses**: Lower % thresholds (3-5%) for tighter control
- **Seasonal businesses**: Compare to prior year same period, not just budget
- **High-growth**: Focus on absolute $ variance, not just %

## Commentary Framework

### Structure for Each Flagged Item
1. **What happened**: State the variance in both $ and % terms
2. **Why it happened**: 2-3 most likely drivers (be specific)
3. **Impact**: Effect on overall P&L or key metrics
4. **Action**: Specific next step with owner and timeline

### Example Commentary
```
Marketing Expense: $90K over budget (+12.0%)

Drivers:
- Digital advertising expansion for Q2 product launch (+$30K)
- Unplanned trade show participation (+$15K)
- New agency retainer for brand refresh (+$15K)
- Video production costs for social campaign (+$5K)
- Misc timing differences (+$25K)

Impact: Reduces EBITDA margin by 1.5pp vs budget

Action: CMO to present ROI analysis for incremental spend at next
budget review. Evaluate whether to reduce Q2 marketing or adjust
full-year forecast.
```

## Executive Summary Structure

### Performance Highlights (Top of Report)
- Revenue vs budget ($ and %)
- EBITDA vs budget ($ and %)
- One-line assessment (ahead/behind/on track)

### Top Variances
- List top 5 unfavorable variances with amounts
- List top 5 favorable variances with amounts
- Each with one-line driver explanation

### Key Action Items
- Numbered list of specific actions
- Include owner and deadline
- Prioritize by impact

### Strategic Questions
- 2-3 questions for leadership to discuss
- Forward-looking (not just backward analysis)

## Trend Analysis

### Tracking Over Time
- Show variance % for each period (monthly or quarterly)
- Add trend indicators:
  - Improving: Variance moving toward budget
  - Worsening: Variance moving away from budget
  - Flat: No meaningful change in variance direction

### Seasonality Adjustments
- Compare to same period last year, not just sequential
- Note seasonal factors in commentary
- Adjust thresholds for known seasonal patterns

## Common Variance Drivers by Category

### Revenue
- Volume changes (units sold)
- Pricing changes (discounts, rate increases)
- Mix shift (high-margin vs low-margin products)
- Timing (deals closed early/late)
- New business vs renewal vs churn

### COGS / Cost of Revenue
- Input cost changes (materials, labor rates)
- Efficiency gains or losses
- Volume impact on fixed cost absorption
- Vendor contract changes

### Operating Expenses
- Headcount changes (new hires, attrition)
- Compensation adjustments (raises, bonuses)
- Discretionary spending (travel, marketing)
- Professional services (consultants, legal)
- Timing differences (prepaid vs accrual)

## Formatting Standards

### Number Formats
- Currency: $1,234K or $1.2M (use K for thousands, M for millions)
- Percentages: One decimal place (5.0%, not 5%)
- Variance: Show sign — $50K favorable, $(50K) unfavorable

### Color Coding
- Green: Favorable variance >5%
- Yellow: Within +/-5% of budget
- Red: Unfavorable variance >5%
- Bold: Any item exceeding materiality threshold

### Report Layout
- Freeze top row and left column
- Group line items by category (Revenue, COGS, OpEx, etc.)
- Show subtotals for each category
- Grand total at bottom
- Commentary column on far right
